## Astro Photography: Setup Guide & Capturing Tips

**Source:** Word document guide (email attachment)  
**Type:** Practical camera settings and technique guide for night sky photography

### Top 10 Tips

#### 1. Shoot RAW
Essential for astro photography — extreme low light conditions require maximum data capture, especially in shadows. JPEG compression loses critical shadow detail.

#### 2. Turn OFF Image Stabilisation
Disable both lens-based and body-based stabilization. Can cause stars to appear streaky if triggered by wind or vibration during long exposures.

#### 3. Exposure Length — The 500 Rule (or 350 for modern cameras)
- **Classic formula:** 500 ÷ focal length (full-frame equivalent) = max seconds before star trails
- **Modern high-res recommendation:** Use 350 instead of 500
- **Example:** At 24mm → 350/24 ≈ 15 seconds

#### 4. Aperture — Stop Down 1-2 Clicks from Maximum
Don't shoot wide open. Stopping down reduces:
- Vignetting (dark corners)
- Coma aberration on outer edges
- Chromatic aberrations
- **Example:** f/1.8 lens → shoot at f/2.0 or f/2.2

#### 5. ISO — Balance Sensitivity vs Noise
Modern cameras handle 6,400+ ISO comfortably; older models struggle above that. Experiment to find your camera's noise floor.

#### 6. Use Remote or Timer
Avoid camera shake from pressing the shutter button. Use a remote trigger or 2-second self-timer.

#### 7. Turn OFF Noise Reduction
Disable both Long Exposure NR and High ISO NR — they create delays that disrupt timing for panoramas and image stacking sequences.

#### 8. Consistent White Balance (4100-4300K)
Set fixed Kelvin balance rather than auto WB:
- Ensures consistent color across nights
- Critical for stitching panoramas without color mismatches
- Allows comparing sky colors between shooting sessions

#### 9. Focusing — The Most Critical Step
- Use Live View, zoom to 10x on a bright star
- Set focus to CLOSE (not infinity) and adjust until star is smallest
- This ensures both stars AND foreground are in focus
- Setting focus to infinity first then backing off misses the sweet spot

#### 10. Check Histogram — Not Preview Screen
Camera preview screens are backlit and misleading. The histogram shows actual RAW data capture:
- Ensure foreground isn't completely black (underexposed)
- Verify shadow detail is being captured
