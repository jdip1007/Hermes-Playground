# Human vs LLM Writing Comparison

Computational analysis reveals systematic differences between human high-quality writing and large language model (LLM) output across linguistic features, factual accuracy, creative capacity, and stylistic variation [1].

## Key Findings

**Linguistic signatures:** LLMs exhibit lower perplexity (45-75 vs 85-120), reduced burstiness (0.3-0.5 vs 0.6-0.9), elevated transition word usage, and flatter syntactic variation distributions [[Stylometry]] [1].

**Factual reliability:** LLM hallucination rates 5-30% depending on domain; humans near-zero for deliberate writing but exhibit bias-driven distortion [[Large language model]] [2][1].

**Creative gaps:** Humans superior in originality, emotional resonance, cultural situatedness. LLMs optimize statistical probability — no true extrapolation beyond training data convex hull [3][1].

**Detection methods:** Multi-feature ensemble classifiers achieve 85-92% accuracy but degrade as models improve. Perplexity/burstiness thresholding most reliable but easily evaded via paraphrasing [4][1].

## Quality Dimensions [1]

| Dimension | Human Strength | LLM Strength |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| Coherence | Global argument structure | Local sentence-level flow |
| Originality | Novel conceptual blending | Pattern recombination speed |
| Depth | Abstract reasoning, nuance | Surface-level synthesis |
| Voice | Idiosyncratic stylistic fingerprint | Statistical average convergence |
| Factual grounding | Source traceability | Probabilistic plausibility |

## Practical Implications [1]

- **Academic:** AI disclosure policies required; detection tools imperfect but improving
- **Professional:** Hybrid workflows (human oversight + AI drafting) optimize efficiency/quality balance
- **Journalism:** Fact-checking protocols essential — hallucination risk non-trivial
