# Human Microbiome

## Overview

The human microbiome comprises all microorganisms (bacteria, archaea, fungi, viruses) inhabiting the human body [1][2]. It contains ~38 trillion microbial cells, roughly matching human cell counts, and harbors ~2-20 million unique microbial genes [2].

## Major Body Sites

- **Gut** — Highest diversity; Firmicutes and Bacteroidetes dominate; critical for metabolism, immunity [1][2]
- **Skin** — Variable by site; Staphylococcus, Corynebacterium, Propionibacterium [2]
- **Oral cavity** — Streptococcus, Prevotella, Veillonella; linked to systemic health [2]
- **Respiratory** — Lower abundance; Haemophilus, Streptococcus, Moraxella [2]
- **Urogenital** — Lactobacillus-dominated in healthy vagina [1][2]

## Health Associations

- **Gut-brain axis** — Microbiome influences mood, cognition, neurological conditions [1]
- **Immune development** — Early-life microbiome shapes immune system maturation [1]
- **Metabolic health** — Linked to obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease [1]
- **Inflammatory bowel disease** — Dysbiosis patterns in Crohn's and ulcerative colitis [1]
- **Antibiotic impact** — Broad-spectrum antibiotics cause lasting microbiome disruption [1]

## Analysis Methods

- **16S rRNA sequencing** — Taxonomic profiling, cost-effective [2]
- **Shotgun metagenomics** — Species/strain-level resolution + functional potential [2]
- **Metatranscriptomics** — Active gene expression in communities [2]
- **Metabolomics** — Microbial metabolite profiling (SCFAs, bile acids) [2]

## Therapeutic Approaches

- **Fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT)** — Effective for recurrent C. difficile [1][2]
- **Probiotics/prebiotics** — Mixed evidence, strain-specific effects [1]
- **Phage therapy** — Targeted reduction of specific pathogens [1]
- **Postbiotics** — Defined microbial metabolites [1]
