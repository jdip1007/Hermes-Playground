## Overview

Method for DNA extraction from *E. coli* as an example of Gram-negative bacteria.[1] Uses QIAGEN Genomic-tip 500/G columns — phenol/chloroform-free with consistently high yield.[1] Chloramphenicol pre-treatment (1 hour before collection) increases yield **4.5×** (from 7.4 µg to 33.9 µg) by stopping cell division while allowing DNA replication completion.[1]

## Key Steps

- Culture *E. coli* to OD ~0.5[1]
- Add chloramphenicol to 180 µg/ml, incubate 1 hour (OD reaches ~0.7)[1]
- Remove 10 ml culture for extraction using QIAGEN Genomic-tip 500/G protocol[1]
- Elute into 200 µl TE buffer pH 8[1]
