# Extended-Spectrum β-Lactamases (ESBL): Overview — Chinese Review Article

## Overview [1]

This is a Chinese-language review article covering the characterization, epidemiology, and detection of extended-spectrum β-lactamases [1]. The article provides a comprehensive overview of ESBL types, their molecular mechanisms, laboratory detection methods, and geographic distribution patterns with emphasis on data from China and Asia-Pacific regions [1].

## Molecular Mechanisms

### β-Lactamase Gene (bla) Characteristics
- ESBL genes are typically **plasmid-borne** and horizontally transferable
- Located on conjugative plasmids that mediate dissemination among pathogenic enterobacteria
- Often co-localized with other resistance determinants (fluoroquinolone, aminoglycoside resistance genes)

### Substrate Activity Profile
ESBLs hydrolyze:
- **Expanded-spectrum cephalosporins**: cefotaxime, ceftriaxone, ceftazidime
- **Penicillins**: including ampicillin, piperacillin
- **Monobactams**: aztreonam

ESBLs do NOT hydrolyze:
- Cephamycins (cefoxitin, cefotetan)
- Carbapenems (imipenem, meropenem)

### Inhibition by β-Lactamase Inhibitors
- ESBL activity is **inhibited by clavulanic acid** — this property distinguishes them from AmpC cephalosporinases and carbapenemases
- Double-disc synergy test (DDST): cefotaxime/ceftazidime + clavulanic acid shows enhanced inhibition zones

## Laboratory Detection Methods

### Phenotypic Methods
1. **Double-Disc Synergy Test (DDST)**: Cefotaxime and ceftazidime discs with and without clavulanic acid — synergy indicates ESBL production
2. **Minimum Inhibitory Concentration (MIC) determination**: Elevated MICs for expanded-spectrum cephalosporins
3. **Isoelectric Focusing (IEF)**: Determines pI value to identify specific ESBL type
4. **Nitrocefin test (CSA)**: Detects β-lactamase activity but does NOT differentiate ESBL from other types

### Genotypic Methods
- **PCR amplification**: Specific primers for blaTEM, blaSHV, blaCTX-M genes
- **DNA sequencing**: Definitive identification of ESBL type and variants
- **Real-time PCR (qPCR)**: Rapid detection with higher throughput

## Epidemiology

### Geographic Distribution Patterns
The article documents ESBL prevalence across multiple regions:

**China/Asia-Pacific**: High diversity of ESBL types reported, including CTX-M-3, CTX-M-14, SHV-5, and TEM variants. Taiwan data shows particularly high rates (CTX-M-14 at 7.9 pI in K. pneumoniae).

### Host Species Affected
ESBL-producing bacteria reported in: Klebsiella spp., Escherichia coli, Salmonella, Proteus, Citrobacter spp., Morganella morganii, Serratia marcescens, Shigella dysenteriae. Also noted in Pseudomonas aeruginosa and Acinetobacter baumannii (though these produce different β-lactamase types).

## Treatment Implications

- ESBL-producing organisms remain susceptible to **carbapenems**
- Co-resistance to fluoroquinolones, aminoglycosides, and other antibiotic classes is common
- Clavulanic acid combination therapy may be effective for some infections but carbapenems preferred for serious infections
