# Emerging & Re-emerging RNA Viruses: Genomic Adaptation

> **Source:** Huang K, Chu JJH (2021). "Editorial: Evolution & Genomic Adaptation of Emerging and Re-emerging RNA Viruses." *Frontiers in Microbiology* 12:777257. DOI: [10.3389/fmicb.2021.777257](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2021.777257)
> **Tags:** virology, RNA viruses, emerging infections, genomic adaptation, SARS-CoV-2

## Overview

This editorial introduces a Research Topic in *Frontiers in Microbiology* collecting 15 peer-reviewed articles on the evolution and genomic adaptation of emerging and re-emerging RNA viruses [1]. The collection covers positive-sense single-strand RNA (+ssRNA) viruses, negative-sense single-strand RNA (-ssRNA) viruses, and diagnostic/host-virus interaction studies.

## Key Themes

### Genomic Alterations Driving Emergence

Three primary mechanisms enable RNA virus emergence [1, 3]:

1. **Spontaneous mutation**: High error rates of viral RNA polymerases generate genetic diversity
2. **Recombination**: Template switching during replication creates novel genomic combinations (e.g., PEDV S1 protein insertion)
3. **Reassortment**: Segmented viruses (influenza) exchange genome segments between strains, driving pandemics

When accumulated changes affect antigenic or receptor-binding regions, host immune systems fail to recognize new variants, enabling global outbreaks [3]. SARS-CoV-2 exemplified this with >4.56 million deaths by late 2021.

### +ssRNA Virus Studies (Baltimore Group IV) [2]

#### Coronaviridae
- **SARS-CoV-2 in China** (Song et al.): Isolated from Henan Province; estimated TMRCA and evolutionary rate
- **SARS-CoV-2 in Brazil** (Resende et al.): 190 isolates from 13 states; B.1.1.33-like viruses possibly transmitted from Europe or domestic emergence; public health interventions reduced median Re by 66%
- **SARS-CoV-2 in Uruguay** (Mir et al.): 122 border-area viruses; multiple independent introductions from Brazil (B.1.1.28, B.1.1.33); synonymous and non-synonymous SNPs define lineages
- **SARS-CoV-2 meta-analysis** (Castonguay et al.): Fourth systematic tracking of evolutionary trajectory, emerging mutations, structural changes, molecular interactions
- **Avian infectious bronchitis virus** (Jiang et al.): Critical mutation determining host tropism alteration — key to understanding cross-species coronavirus transmission
- **Porcine Epidemic Diarrhea Virus** (Li et al.): Unique S1 protein insertion via recombination; predicted structure showed higher pathogenicity in piglet challenge

#### Picornaviridae
- **Norovirus GII.17** (Zuo et al.): New variants surpassed predominant GII.4 genotype causing Kawasaki outbreaks (2014–2015); weak cross-protection identified; mutated amino acids on antigenic sites mapped
- **Norovirus GII.2** (Li et al.): Eight distinct clusters with increased genetic diversity but no elevated evolutionary rate; selection pressure detected, suggesting outbreak unrelated to evolutionary adaptation
- **Coxsackievirus A16** (Nhu et al.): First molecular epidemiology study in Southern Vietnam; single genogroup B1a; less pronounced genetic alteration vs. EV-A71

#### Flaviviridae & Togaviridae
- **Zika virus**: Review of amino acid substitutions contributing to pathogenicity and transmission
- **Chikungunya virus** (Sharif et al.): Systematic review of evolution, epidemiology, phylogeny

### -ssRNA Virus Studies (Baltimore Group V) [2]

#### Influenza
- **Gene reassortment** (Trifkovic et al.): Vaccine seed production model revealed selective preference for genome compatibility between different viruses; additional driving force beyond initial hypotheses
- **Canine influenza** (Guo et al.): 54 substituted amino acids fixed during avian-to-canine interspecies transmission; selection pressure and codon usage analysis supports avian origin

### Diagnostic & Host-Virus Interaction Studies [1]

- **RVFV detection** (Han et al.): Isothermal amplification assay using conservative RNA gene segment; ~200 copies/µL sensitivity (100× more sensitive than RT-PCR); no cross-reactivity; results in 5 minutes, no specialized equipment needed
- **Host-virus network dynamics** (Romano et al.): First application of Systems Thinking framework to host-virus interactive networks; System Dynamics modeling identified leverage points to minimize virion release

## Critical Analysis

### Strengths [1]

1. **Comprehensive scope**: Covers major RNA virus families (+ssRNA, -ssRNA) with both empirical studies and reviews
2. **Geographic diversity**: Studies span China, Brazil, Uruguay, Vietnam — important for understanding global viral evolution patterns
3. **Practical relevance**: Includes diagnostic development (RVFV assay) alongside fundamental evolutionary research
4. **Timely context**: Published during SARS-CoV-2 pandemic with direct relevance to variant tracking

### Limitations [1]

1. **Editorial format**: Summarizes rather than presents original data; detailed methodology and results require consulting individual articles
2. **Selection bias**: 15 of 28 submitted manuscripts accepted — rejected studies may contain important negative findings
3. **Limited mechanistic depth**: Editorial overview cannot provide the molecular detail needed for understanding specific adaptation mechanisms

### Clinical Relevance [1, 3]

- SARS-CoV-2 variant tracking frameworks directly applicable to pandemic preparedness
- Norovirus GII.17 emergence demonstrates that non-SARS coronaviruses also undergo significant antigenic evolution requiring surveillance
- RVFV diagnostic assay represents deployable technology for emerging pathogen detection in resource-limited settings
- Canine influenza adaptation data informs understanding of interspecies transmission barriers
