## Overview

Phenol-chloroform based method to purify HMW genomic DNA embedded and stored in 1% low-melting point agarose plugs.[1] Validated with Lambda DNA and *S. cerevisiae* DNA in agarose plugs.[1]

## Key Steps

- Transfer agarose plug (~3–6 µg DNA) to 2 ml tube, add 200–400 µl TE buffer + NaCl to ~200 mM final[1]
- Melt agarose at 70°C until transparent and homogeneous (~5 minutes)[1]
- Phenol extraction: add 1 volume phenol, rotate on HulaMixer for 2 hours at room temperature in fume hood[1]
- Centrifuge 5 min at 9400 × g, transfer supernatant to new tube[1]
- Repeat with chloroform, then precipitate DNA with ammonium acetate and ethanol[1]
