# Senotherapeutics for Skin Aging

## Overview

Senotherapeutics target cellular senescence — a state of irreversible cell cycle arrest accompanied by pro-inflammatory secretory phenotype (SASP) [1,2,3]. Two complementary strategies exist: **senolytics** eliminate senescent cells via apoptosis induction, while **senomorphics** suppress SASP without killing the cells [2,3]. Both approaches show promise for skin rejuvenation and systemic anti-aging effects [1,3].

## Senolytic Therapies

### Dasatinib + Quercetin (D+Q)

The most studied senolytic combination [2,3]:
- Reduces p16INK4A and p21CIP1-positive cells in human skin epidermis [3]
- Decreases proportion of SASP proteins [2,3]
- Shown to reduce senescent cells in humans with diabetic kidney disease (Hickson et al., EBioMedicine 2019) [3]
- In vitro/in vivo skin rejuvenation demonstrated via selective elimination of senescent cells (Takaya & Kishi, Biogerontology 2024) [2]

### Other Senolytic Candidates

- **Fisetin:** Natural flavonoid with senolytic activity in multiple tissue types [2,3]
- **Navitoclax (ABT-263):** Bcl-2 family inhibitor; effective but off-target platelet toxicity limits use [2,3]
- **Quercetin derivatives:** Various formulations under investigation [2]

## Senomorphic Therapies

### Rapamycin (Sirolimus)

mTORC1 inhibitor with well-documented senomorphic effects [2,3]:
- Inhibits IL-1α upregulation and other SASP components in dermal fibroblasts [2]
- Reduces SA-β-gal positive cells (senescence marker) [3]
- Topical rapamycin reduced aging markers (p16INK4A) and restored collagen VII in human skin clinical trial (Chung et al., Geroscience 2019) [3]
- Protects skin fibroblasts from UVB-induced photoaging by suppressing ROS production (Qin et al., Cell Physiol Biochem 2018) [2]

**Systemic effects:** Phase 2b/3 trials show rapamycin improves immune function in older adults and targets accelerated cardiovascular aging (Mannick et al., Lancet Healthy Longev 2021; Nat Aging 2023) [1].

### Other Senomorphics

- **Metformin:** AMPK activator with mild senomorphic properties [2,3]
- **Acetyl-L-carnitine + Ellagic acid derivatives (EAS-45-4):** Suppresses SASP in multiple cell types [2]

## NAD+ Boosters

NAD+ decline is a central feature of aging, affecting mitochondrial function, sirtuin activity, and DNA repair [1,4]. Several precursors are under investigation:

### Nicotinamide Riboside (NR)

- Rescues melanocyte stem cell decline in aged mice — increases mast/stem cell growth factor receptor kit and short TRP channel 2 expression (Zhang et al., Science 2016) [4]
- Beneficially affects muscle and neural stem cells → increased lifespan in mouse models [4]
- Activates sirtuins (SIRT1, SIRT3) → improves mitochondrial function [4]

### Nicotinamide Mononucleotide (NMN)

- Clinical trials show safety and antiaging effects including improved vascular function (Song et al., Adv Nutr 2023) [4]
- NAD+ role in cellular energy generation, redox reactions, and signaling pathways regulating healthspan (Bhasin et al., Endocr Rev 2023) [4]

### Nicotinamide (NAM/Niacinamide)

- Amide form of vitamin B3; precursor to NAD+ and NADPH [4]
- Multifaceted role in skin health: DNA repair support, anti-inflammatory effects, barrier function improvement (Camillo et al., Medicina 2025; Marques et al., Antioxidants 2024) [1,4]

### PDRN (Polydeoxyribonucleotide)

- Prevents SIRT1 degradation by attenuating autophagy during skin aging [1]
- Exhibits antioxidant and anti-aging properties via enhanced SIRT1 expression (Chen et al., PLoS One 2025) [1]

## Mitochondrial Medicines

### Urolithin A

- Natural metabolite produced by gut microbiota from ellagitannins [1,2]
- Induces mitophagy → improves mitochondrial quality control [2]
- Protects human dermal fibroblasts from UVA-induced photoaging via NRF2 activation and mitophagy (Liu et al., J Photochem Photobiol B 2022) [2]
- Clinical benefits in animal models and humans with various age-related disorders [1,2]

### Autophagy Inducers

- **Anacardic acid, curcumin, garcinol:** Inhibit EP300 acetyltransferase → induce autophagy + inhibit mTORC1 (Pietrocola et al., Cell Death Differ 2015) [2]
- **Spermidine:** Natural polyamine; induces autophagy via EP300 inhibition [2]

## Telomerase Activation

- In telomerase-deficient mice, telomerase activation addresses aging-associated skin changes without notably increasing carcinogenesis risk (Siegl-Cachedenier et al., J Cell Biol 2007) [1]
- Protecting melanocyte telomeres against oxidative stress may delay skin aging and prevent accelerated biological aging systemically [1]
- **Caveat:** Human skin chronically exposed to UVR differs from laboratory mouse models — malignancy risk cannot be fully ruled out [1]

## Oral Supplements for Skin Aging

Narrative review of oral supplementation approaches (Morgado-Carrasco et al., Actas Dermo-Sifiliograficas 2023) [1]:
- Collagen peptides: Daily 5g supplementation positively affects visible signs of skin and nail aging in East Asian population (Vleminckx et al., J Cosmet Dermatol 2024) [1]
- Antioxidants: Vitamin C, E, polyphenols reduce oxidative stress markers [1]
- Omega-3 fatty acids: Anti-inflammatory effects on skin barrier function [1]

## Current Challenges

1. **Biomarker limitations:** p16INK4A widely used but not all senescence is p16-dependent; multi-marker approaches needed [2,3]
2. **Delivery methods:** Topical vs systemic delivery trade-offs for different targets [3]
3. **Individual variation:** Skin type differences suggest treatments may vary among individuals [1]
4. **Long-term safety:** Senolytic clearance of beneficial senescent cells (e.g., in wound healing) remains a concern [2,3]
