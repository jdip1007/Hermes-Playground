# Yi Guan

**Related:** [Pandemic Influenza Emergence](concepts/pandemic-influenza-emergence.md), [Robert G Webster](entities/robert-g-webster.md)

## Overview

Yi Guan is a virologist specializing in emerging infectious diseases, particularly influenza and coronaviruses. Based at the University of Hong Kong's Li Ka Shing Faculty of Medicine. Corresponding author on landmark studies dating pandemic influenza emergence using molecular clock methods.

## Key Contributions

- Led Bayesian relaxed molecular clock analysis showing 1918 H1N1 components circulated in mammals as early as 1911, challenging the "direct avian introduction" hypothesis [1]
- Demonstrated that seasonal and classic swine H1N1 were not derived directly from BM/1918 but co-circulated during the pandemic [1]
- Established framework for using TMRCA estimates to infer interspecies transmission timelines across all 8 influenza gene segments [1]

## Affiliations (at time of Smith et al. 2009)
