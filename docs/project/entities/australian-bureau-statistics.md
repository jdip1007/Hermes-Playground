## Overview

The Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) is Australia's national statistical agency and an official source of independent, reliable information[1]. It collates and analyses statistical data on economic, demographic, environmental and social issues to support policy research and development[2]. The ABS reports to the federal Treasurer through the Australian Statistician[3].

The bureau's function originated in the Commonwealth Bureau of Census and Statistics, established under the Census and Statistics Act 1905, four years after the Federation of Australia[4]. The organisation took on its present name in 1975 when the Australian Bureau of Statistics Act 1975 replaced the CBCS[5].

## History

- **1905**: Commonwealth Bureau of Census and Statistics (CBCS) established under the Census and Statistics Act 1905, with Sir George Handley Knibbs as first Commonwealth Statistician[6]
- **1911**: First national census conducted across all Australian states, deploying approximately 7,300 collectors[7]
- **1928**: Bureau relocated from Melbourne to Canberra[8]
- **1932**: CBCS subsumed within the Department of the Treasury[9]
- **Late 1950s**: Final unification of all state statistical offices with CBCS under Sir Stanley Carver[10]
- **1974**: CBCS abolished and replaced by Australian Bureau of Statistics[11]
- **2015**: $250 million five-year government investment announced to modernise ABS systems and processes[12]

## Census Operations

The ABS conducts the Australian Census of Population and Housing every five years as stipulated under federal law in the Constitution of Australia[13]. The census aims to accurately measure the population, number of dwellings, and key characteristics across Australia[14].

**Recent Censuses:**
- **2016**: First largely online census; experienced 5 distributed denial-of-service attacks causing a 43-hour outage on census night[15]
- **2021**: Conducted during COVID-19 pandemic; achieved 96.1% dwelling response rate (up from 95.1% in 2016), collecting data from 10,852,208 dwellings[16]
- **2026**: Planned to incorporate administrative data integration for improved efficiency and coverage[17]

Census data is used for defining electoral boundaries, planning infrastructure, establishing community services, and formulating public policy[18].

## Flagship Publications

The ABS publishes monthly and quarterly economic information spanning interest rates, property prices, employment, the value of the Australian dollar, and commodity prices[19]. Key publications include:

**Economic Indicators:**
- Consumer Price Index (CPI) - measures inflation through price changes across a basket of goods and services[20]
- Labour Force statistics - employment and unemployment data[21]
- Australian National Accounts - GDP, national income, expenditure flows[22]
- Average Weekly Earnings[23]
- Key Economic Indicators composite measure[24]

**Social Statistics:**
- Australian Health Survey (includes biomedical components with blood/urine testing and food diaries)[25]
- National Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Social Survey (NATSISS)[26]
- Crime statistics covering offenders, victims, corrections system and prisons[27]
- Demographic releases: population growth, projections, migration, births/deaths[28]

**Other Major Areas:**
- Education: Childhood Education and Care, Schools, Education and Work publications[29]
- Environment: Energy/water use, conservation activities, land management data[30]
- Research & Innovation: R&D expenditure surveys conducted since 1978[31]

## Data Services

The ABS provides multiple pathways for accessing statistical data:

**Data Integration:** The process of combining data from two or more sources at the unit level (person and/or business)[32]. Integrated data enhances insights into society, economy and environment to support research and improve government policies in health, education, infrastructure, and business[33].

**Access Frameworks:**
- **Five Safes Framework**: Used to manage safe and secure access to integrated data through highly secure systems[34]
- **Accredited Data Service Provider**: ABS works as a trusted partner with government, research institutions and business on data integration projects with clear public benefit[35]

**Data Access Tools:**
- TableBuilder - custom table creation from ABS microdata[36]
- Secure Environment for Analysing Data (SEAD) - controlled access to sensitive datasets[37]
- Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) - programmatic data access[38]
- Microdata access through accredited researchers[39]

## Methodology Standards

The ABS maintains rigorous methodology standards including:

**Australian Statistical Geography Standard (ASGS):** A system of geographic statistical areas in Australia, currently at Version 3 (2021)[40]. The ASGS includes:
- Main Structure - nested hierarchy from small units to whole of Australia[41]
- Indigenous Structure for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander populations[42]
- Urban Areas structure[43]
- Remoteness Structure[44]

**Edition 4 (2026) planned changes:**
- Urban Centres and Localities designed using Mesh Blocks rather than Statistical Area Level 1[45]
- Removal of Australian Drainage Divisions and Tourism Regions from Non ABS Structure[46]
- Digital boundary files released using Geocentric Datum of Australia 2020 (GDA2020)[47]

**Quality Frameworks:** The ABS follows international statistical standards through engagement with UN Statistical Commission, OECD Committee on Statistics and Statistical Policy, and UNECE Conference for European Statisticians[48].

## International Engagement

The ABS participates in:
- United Nations Statistical Commission (UNSC)[49]
- Organisation for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) Committee on Statistics and Statistical Policy (CSSP)[50]
- United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UNECE) Conference for European Statisticians[51]

Partnership with DFAT to deliver statistical capability building programs for Indo-Pacific region, including in-country training and hosting development visits from countries including China, Japan, Canada, Korea, and Nepal[52].

## Leadership

The head of ABS is the Australian Statistician (previously Commonwealth Statistician), reporting to the federal Treasurer[53]. Current incumbent since 11 December 2019: David Gruen. Previous holders include David Kalisch and Brian Pink[54].
