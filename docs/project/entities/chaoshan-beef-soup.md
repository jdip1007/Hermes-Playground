## Chaoshan Beef Soup (潮汕牛肉汤)

A traditional dish from eastern Guangdong province[1]. The narrator calls it **one of Asia's three famous soups** (亚洲三大名汤之一)[1]. Every Chaoshan mother has her own version — this tutorial shares the family recipe without reservation[1].

### Ingredients

* **Beef** — 吊龙 (diào lóng, brisket flap) recommended; lean with even fat distribution, ideal for clear soup
* **Ginger** — sliced
* **Scallions** — chopped (葱花)
* **Cilantro** (香菜)
* **Celery** — cut into segments (芹菜段)
* **Garlic oil** — pre-made crispy garlic oil with whole garlic pieces (蒜头酥油)[1]
* **Green vegetables** — for children (青菜)
* **Salt**, light soy sauce (生抽), cooking oil

### Method

1. **Cut the beef** — Slice against the grain, cutting through all fat fibers; cut thin for tender texture[1]. The old saying: "切牛肉要逆纹" (cut beef against the grain)
2. **Do NOT salt or marinate** — Many people make this mistake. Salting or adding cooking wine before blanching masks the beef's natural sweetness and freshness[1]
3. **Lock in moisture** — Add just one spoonful of cooking oil to the cut beef to seal in nutrients and moisture[1]
4. **Prepare soup base** — In a bowl: scallions, cilantro, celery segments, salt, light soy sauce for flavor[1]
5. **Add garlic oil** — A small amount of crispy garlic oil elevates freshness; the narrator recommends pre-made 蒜头酥油 with clean ingredients and whole garlic pieces packed inside[1]. Versatile: use for soups, steamed wontons (蒸云吞), tossed noodles (拌面), rice rolls (肠粉)
6. **Blanch vegetables** — Boil water with a little oil, add green vegetables until cooked through, remove and place in bowl[1]
7. **Blanch beef** — In the same boiling water, add marinated beef. Watch timing carefully: overcooking ruins texture and flavor[1]. Just 10 seconds after water returns to boil is enough (水开后烫个10秒断生)[1]
8. **Finish with broth** — Don't discard the blanching water; it's concentrated with vegetable essence[1]. Skim all foam clean (撇净浮沫), pour into bowl over beef and vegetables[1]

### Key Principles

* **Clear broth** — The final soup should be clear and transparent (汤色清亮通透), refreshing and savory (清爽鲜美)[1]
* **Salt last** — Adding salt or cooking wine early masks the natural sweetness of the beef[1]
* **Quick blanching** — 10 seconds is enough; overcooking makes beef tough and flavorless[1]
* **Cut against grain** — Always slice perpendicular to muscle fibers for tenderness[1]
* **Garlic oil** — Pre-made crispy garlic oil adds depth without clouding the broth[1]

### Cultural Context

Chaoshan beef soup represents the Chaoshan culinary philosophy: superior ingredients need minimal seasoning. The dish is closely related to Chaoshan beef hotpot (潮汕牛肉火锅), but focuses on a soup/broth preparation rather than the communal hotpot dining style. The key distinguishing feature is the emphasis on clear, clean broth over complex spice profiles.

The narrator closes with: "每次做这道菜，都会想起小时候妈妈的味道" — Every time I make this dish, it reminds me of my mother's cooking from childhood[1].
