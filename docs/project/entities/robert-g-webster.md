# Robert G. Webster

**Related:** [Pandemic Influenza Emergence](concepts/pandemic-influenza-emergence.md), [Yi Guan](entities/yi-guan.md)

## Overview

Robert G. Webster is a virologist and one of the most influential influenza researchers of the modern era. Known as the "father of influenza" for his foundational work on influenza virus evolution, antigenic drift/shift mechanisms, and the role of aquatic birds as the natural reservoir for all influenza A viruses.

## Key Contributions

- Co-discovered that **all influenza A viruses originate from wild aquatic birds** (the global reservoir) [1]
- Defined the concept of **antigenic shift** — reassortment between avian and human strains producing pandemic viruses [1]
- Established swine as **"mixing vessels"** for influenza reassortment due to dual receptor specificity (α2,3 and α2,6 sialic acids) [1]
- Contributed to understanding the 1957 H2N2 and 1968 H3N2 pandemics as reassortment events between avian and human viruses [1]

## Affiliations (at time of Smith et al. 2009)

Virology Division, Department of Infectious Diseases, St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, Memphis TN. Contributed to PNAS paper on dating pandemic influenza emergence. [1]
