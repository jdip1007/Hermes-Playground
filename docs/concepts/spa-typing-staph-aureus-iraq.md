# Spa Typing of Staphylococcus aureus from Iraq Outpatients

## Overview

A molecular epidemiology study characterizing **spa types** of *Staphylococcus aureus* isolates from outpatients in southern Iraq (Basrah and Thi-Qar provinces). The study identified 11 distinct spa types among 36 sequenced isolates, with **t304** (30.3%) and **t307/t037** (19.4%) as predominant types — several reported for the first time in Iraq. Found an exceptionally high MRSA prevalence of **95.4%** among 65 *S. aureus* isolates from 290 clinical specimens.

## Study Design

### Population and Sampling
- **290 clinical specimens** from outpatients or patients upon admission
- Locations: Basrah and Thi-Qar provinces, southern Iraq
- Exclusion criteria: Recent hospital admission, hemodialysis patients, recent surgery, intravenous cannula at swab time
- Sample types: Urine (n=34), wound swabs (n=9), nasal swabs (n=7), tonsil swabs (n=6), burn swabs (n=5), blood (n=2), sputum/pleural aspirates (n=2)

### Methods
- **Species identification**: Standard microbiological techniques + 16S rRNA PCR confirmation (228 bp fragment, Monday & Bohach primers)
- **Antibiotic susceptibility**: Kirby-Bauer disk diffusion (methicillin 10 µg, oxacillin 1 µg, vancomycin 30 µg) per CLSI 2019 guidelines; VITEK 2 for vancomycin MIC
- **mecA detection**: PCR amplification of 310 bp product (Geha et al. primers)
- **PVL detection**: lukS-PV-lukF-PV gene screening (Lina et al. primers)
- **spa typing**: PCR amplification of spa X region variable repeats + Sanger sequencing (Macrogen, Seoul); analysis via Ridom SpaServer and spatyper.fortinbras.us

## Key Results

### Prevalence and Resistance Profile
| Parameter | Result |
|---|---|
| *S. aureus* isolation rate | 65/290 (22.4%) |
| MRSA prevalence | 62/65 (95.4%) |
| mecA-positive among MRSA | 60/62 (96.8%) |
| Vancomycin resistance | 5/65 (7.7%) |
| PVL gene positive | 14/65 (21.5%) |

### Spa Type Distribution (n = 36 sequenced)
- **t304**: 12 isolates (30.3%) — predominant in Thi-Qar province, mostly MRSA
- **t037/t307**: 7 isolates (19.4%) — predominant in Basrah province, all methicillin-resistant
- **t346**: 3 isolates (8.3%)
- **t044**: 3 isolates (8.3%)
- **t15595**: 3 isolates (8.3%)
- **t386**: 2 isolates (5.5%)
- **t5475**: 2 isolates (5.5%) — very rare globally, MSSA in this study
- **t17928**: 1 isolate (2.8%) — MRSA (only prior record: MSSA from Sweden)
- **t14870**: 1 isolate (2.8%)
- **t021**: 1 isolate (2.8%)
- **t024**: 1 isolate (2.8%)

### Phylogenetic Clustering
- **Clade A** (91.7%, n=33): t304, t037, t024, t044, t346, t021, t15595, t5475, t17928
- **Clade B** (8.3%, n=3): t386, t14870 — possibly from patient mobility from other Iraqi regions

### GenBank Deposits
Accession numbers: LC577038–LC577073 (isolates KAZ1–KAZ37) and LC586070.1 (KAZ7.1)

## Notable Findings

### mecA Discordance
- **2 MRSA isolates were mecA-negative**: Suggests alternative methicillin resistance mechanisms (e.g., enhanced PBP2a expression, efflux pumps) — consistent with Ba et al. 2014 findings
- **1 MSSA isolate was mecA-positive**: Possibly inactive mecA due to mutation (Kuwahara-Arai et al. 1996)
- Implication: mecA presence/absence alone is insufficient for MRSA/MSSA characterization

### Regional Epidemiology
- **t037** dominance in Basrah aligns with patterns in neighboring Iran and Asian countries — likely cross-border patient mobility (Iraq-Iran medical tourism corridor)
- **t304** dominance in Thi-Qar: Rare globally (only 5 countries, small numbers), but dominant locally — suggests a localized endemic clone

### First Reports for Iraq
All identified spa types were reported for the first time in Iraq. Many not previously documented in neighboring countries either, possibly reflecting post-2003 invasion migration patterns and cross-border healthcare-seeking behavior.

## Methodology Assessment

### Strengths
- Combined phenotypic (disk diffusion) + genotypic (mecA PCR) resistance characterization
- Sanger sequencing of spa X region for definitive typing
- Use of established databases (Ridom SpaServer, spatyper.fortinbras.us)
- GenBank deposition enables future comparative analysis

### Limitations
- **Small sample size** for sequencing (36/65 isolates typed) — limits statistical power
- **Single-center study** from two provinces in southern Iraq — may not represent national epidemiology
- **spa typing alone** provides limited resolution compared to whole-genome sequencing (WGS) or MLST
- **No clinical outcome data** linked to spa types
- **Cross-sectional design** — cannot track temporal trends

## Clinical Relevance

### Infection Control Implications
- Exceptionally high MRSA rate (95.4%) warrants enhanced infection control measures in southern Iraq healthcare settings
- Cross-border patient mobility is a significant factor in MRSA spread — regional surveillance coordination needed
- Presence of vancomycin-resistant isolates (7.7%) signals emerging multidrug resistance

### Surveillance Applications
- spa typing provides rapid, reproducible strain discrimination for outbreak investigation
- Results establish baseline epidemiological data for future monitoring
- GenBank deposits enable global comparison via Ridom SpaServer
